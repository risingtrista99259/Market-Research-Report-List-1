<p><p>The organic coir market primarily consists of two types: white fiber and brown fiber. White fiber, derived from the husk of coconuts, is known for its soft texture and is used in products like mats, ropes, and brushes. It is often processed to remove color and impurities, resulting in a cleaner finish. Brown fiber, on the other hand, retains its natural color and is coarser, commonly used in horticulture, erosion control, and industrial applications due to its durability and strength.</p></p>
