<p><ul><li>Water-Hardening Class</li><li>Cold-Work Class</li><li>Shock-Resisting Class</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The high-speed tool steel market consists of several classes, including water-hardening, cold-work, and shock-resisting types. Water-hardening class steels offer excellent toughness and are typically used for tools and dies that require hardening in water. Cold-work class steels are designed for high wear resistance and are commonly used in punching, stamping, and cutting applications. Shock-resisting class steels are engineered to withstand high impact, making them ideal for applications requiring durability and resistance to fracturing under sudden loads.</p></p>
